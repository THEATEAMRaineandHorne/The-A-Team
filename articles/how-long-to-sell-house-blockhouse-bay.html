How Long Does It Take To Sell A House In Blockhouse Bay?

Thinking of putting your home on the market? Here is how long properties are currently taking to sell in Blockhouse Bay, what drives those numbers, and how to speed up your own sale.

By Edita Andrijasevic | Raine & Horne • 2026 market guide for Blockhouse Bay homeowners

If you are thinking about selling in Blockhouse Bay, one of the first questions you probably have is simple: how long is this going to take?

Understanding the average days on market helps you plan your next move, manage your finances and set realistic expectations. Here is a clear breakdown of how long homes are currently taking to sell in Blockhouse Bay, what is driving those timelines, and what you can do to sell faster.

The short answer

According to REINZ data, the median time to sell a house in Blockhouse Bay is currently around 41 days.

That means from the day your listing goes live on Realestate.co.nz or Trade Me Property, it takes a little over six weeks, on average, to secure a signed sale and purchase agreement. Over the last 12 months the median has sat at 42 days, so the figure is consistent and keeps Blockhouse Bay closely aligned with the wider Auckland market.

For your own property, you can request a free Blockhouse Bay appraisal for a more specific likely timeline.

Days on market is only part of the timeline

Days on market measures the time it takes to go under contract. It does not include the settlement period, which usually runs four to six weeks.

So for a realistic end-to-end picture, from your first open home to handing over the keys, it is sensible to budget for roughly 11 to 15 weeks. Some homes move much faster, and some take longer. The medians are a useful guide, not a guarantee.

Blockhouse Bay market snapshot

Recent REINZ figures give a clear picture of the current Blockhouse Bay market:

  • Median sale price: around $1.09 million
  • Median days to sell: about 41 days (42 days over the last 12 months)
  • Sales activity: roughly 219 sales over the past year
  • Price trend: up about 2% on a year ago, but down around 6% on three years ago and 9% on five years ago

The takeaway for sellers is that Blockhouse Bay remains an active, well-traded suburb, but it is no longer the rapidly rising market of a few years ago. That makes accurate pricing and strong presentation more important than ever for achieving a result on the faster side of the median.

Why do some Blockhouse Bay homes sell faster than others?

The 41-day median is a helpful benchmark, but individual results vary widely. In the local West Auckland market, properties generally fall into two distinct speed brackets.

1. The fast trackers (1 to 3 weeks)

Homes that sell well ahead of the average usually share a few traits:

  • The right method of sale: a well-run auction campaign creates natural urgency and a clear deadline for buyers.
  • Flawless presentation: homes that are professionally staged, decluttered and have strong street appeal attract immediate interest.
  • Competitive pricing: properties priced accurately from day one capture active buyers who already know local market value.

2. The slow burners (8+ weeks)

If a property sits on the market for more than eight weeks (well beyond the 41-day median), it is usually due to:

  • Overpricing: testing the market with a price that is too high often leaves a listing looking stale.
  • No price guide: buyers can hesitate to offer when there is no pricing guidance, which lengthens the campaign.
  • Niche appeal: unusual layouts, heavy maintenance needs or unconsented work all shrink the pool of eligible buyers.

The seller takeaway

The biggest factors you control are pricing, presentation and method of sale. Get those three right and you give your home the best chance of selling on the faster side of the average.

3 tips to beat the average and sell faster

If you want to sell your Blockhouse Bay home on the faster side of the 41-day median, focus on these three strategies.

1. Consider the auction advantage

Auctions remain one of the most effective ways to reduce days on market in Auckland. A focused campaign compresses the marketing timeline into a concentrated three-week period and gives buyers a clear deadline to act.

If you want to understand how this works, read our guide to property auctions in West Auckland.

2. Invest in presentation

First impressions matter, and most start online. Professional staging, a fresh coat of neutral paint and a tidy garden can make a property instantly more appealing in photos and at open homes.

For more on this, see our guide on how to prepare your home for a stronger sale price.

3. Trust local data

Do not rely only on generic city-wide figures. Work with an agent who understands the local area, knows the value of in-zone schools such as Blockhouse Bay Intermediate and Lynfield College, and knows what local buyers are actually paying. School zones are a strong driver of buyer demand in Blockhouse Bay, with a large share of properties falling within these popular zones.

What is the best method of sale for a faster result?

There is no single answer that suits every home. Auction, deadline sale, price by negotiation and tender each suit different properties, buyer groups and market conditions.

An auction often suits homes with broad appeal and strong buyer competition. A deadline sale can suit properties where you want a firm timeframe without a public auction. The right choice depends on your property, your timeframe and the likely buyers.
